# Sales & Profit Performance Dashboard (Power BI)

## Project Overview

This project presents an interactive **Tableau dashboard** designed to analyze product sales performance, profit trends, and regional distribution across multiple markets.

The dashboard enables business stakeholders to monitor profitability, understand product performance, and identify trends in monthly sales.

## Business Problem

Companies selling multiple products across different countries need a clear view of:

• Which products generate the most profit  
• Which markets perform best  
• Monthly sales and profit trends  
• Product-level investment vs return  

Without a centralized dashboard, these insights are difficult to track.

This dashboard consolidates operational sales data into a **single analytical view**.

---

## Key Metrics

The dashboard tracks several important KPIs:

• Total Capital Invested  
• Total Profit Generated  
• Total Units Sold  
• Profit Margin (%)  

Additional analytics include:

• Product profit by country  
• Monthly profit trends  
• Invested capital by product  
• Profit distribution by quarter  
• Quantity sold vs profit by product

## Dashboard Features

### Product Profit by Country
Displays how different products perform across various countries, helping identify strong markets for each product.

### Monthly Profit Trend
Shows profit fluctuations throughout the year to identify seasonal trends.

### Capital Investment by Product
Highlights which products require the highest investment.

### Profit Distribution by Quarter
Analyzes quarterly performance and profitability.

### Quantity vs Profit Analysis
Compares sales volume with profit contribution across products.

---

## Key Insights

• Chocolate Chip generated the highest profit across most countries.

• Profit trends show noticeable fluctuations across months, with strong performance in the mid and late parts of the year.

• Some products show high sales volume but relatively lower profit margins.

• Investment levels vary significantly across products, suggesting different cost structures.
